Have Fun in Ann Arbor

Local Ann Arbor Fun

Ann Arbor Art Fair

Dates: July 17–19, 2025

Location: Downtown Ann Arbor

Experience the nation's largest juried art fair, featuring nearly 1,000 artists across three unique fairs: The Original Street Art Fair, The Guild’s Summer Art Fair, and the State Street District Art Fair. Enjoy live music, food vendors, and family-friendly activities throughout the event. Explore the art fair

Ann Arbor Summer Festival

Dates: June–July 2025

Location: Various venues in Ann Arbor

Enjoy a month-long celebration of the arts with performances ranging from music and dance to comedy and theater. The festival features both indoor and outdoor events, including the popular Top of the Park series with free movies and live music. Explore the festival

Things to Do On-Campus

Nichols Arboretum & Peony Garden

Location: 1610 Washington Heights, Ann Arbor

Explore 128 acres of diverse landscapes, including the renowned Peony Garden, which boasts the largest collection of heirloom peonies in North America. The Arboretum offers scenic walking trails, river views, and seasonal blooms. Visit the Arboretum

Matthaei Botanical Gardens

Location: 1800 N Dixboro Rd, Ann Arbor

Discover a variety of themed gardens, including the Great Lakes Garden and the Bonsai Collection. The Botanical Gardens also host seasonal events such as plant sales and educational programs. Explore the Gardens

University of Michigan Museum of Art

Location: Nichols Arboretum

The U-M Museum of Art puts art and ideas at the center of campus and public life. Visit to explore experiences that enrich our understanding of one another, foster joy, and build a more just future. Engage in exhibitions, programs, research, and community partnerships unique to a campus art museum. Visit the UMMA

Kelsey Museum of Archaeology

Location: 434 S State St, Ann Arbor

Explore over 100,000 artifacts from ancient civilizations at this museum located on the University of Michigan campus. The museum offers exhibits and educational programs for visitors of all ages. Visit the Kelsey Museum
